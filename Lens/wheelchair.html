wheelchair dance stage set

to understand the concept of ramps and inclines for our Mechanics Physics class, we partnered with wheelchair-user and dancer alice sheppard, as well as design professor sara hendren. alice dreamed of a performance space that would allow her to showcase and exemplify the magic and art of wheelchairs in dance. she wanted a stage set that was modular, portable, easily manufacturable, and that could open opportunities for her routine that normal, flat stages couldn't.

CAMPUS AUDIT

alice flew in frequently so we could collaborate in person with her and collect her feedback. during one of the first visits, we took her on an audit around different types of ramps and slopes on Olin's campus to gauge her comfort and mastery on various inclines.

MATERIAL

not only was the shape of the ramp important to take into consideration while designing the stage set, but so was the type of material it would be made out of. we had a team focused on researching different coefficients of frictions of different materials, and had alice test which one was optimal for her.

FINAL PROTOTYPE

while we had numerous mini ramps as our initial prototypes, we needed to construct something that was more to-scale, more life size, so that alice and her partner could explore the full range and depth of our design. we hackathoned for over a full weekend to pull this set together in time for her last visit.

TO BE CONTINUED

alice and sara hendren are now working on fabricating this ramp design on a full-scale, top-quality level so that alice can perform on it in the coming year.
